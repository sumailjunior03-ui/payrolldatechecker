What counts as a business day?
A business day is Monday–Friday excluding observed U.S. federal holidays. If a fixed-date holiday lands on a weekend, the observed day is used (Fri/Mon).

What This Calculator Does

This payroll processing date calculator estimates when payroll may be completed based on a payroll submission date and a processing time measured in business days.

Many payroll providers and banks only process payments on business days. If processing falls on a weekend or a U.S. federal holiday (observed), the expected pay date may shift to the next available business day.

This tool provides an estimate for planning and scheduling.

Payroll Processing Formula

Estimated Pay Date = Submission Date + Processing Business Days

A business day is Monday–Friday excluding observed U.S. federal holidays.

Calculation Methodology

  1. The calculator starts from the payroll submission date.
  2. It adds the selected number of business days.
  3. Weekends (Saturday and Sunday) are skipped automatically.
  4. Observed U.S. federal holidays are also skipped.
  5. If the resulting date lands on a non-business day, the calculator moves forward to the next available business day.

This tool provides an estimate. Actual timing depends on your payroll provider, bank settlement rules, and cutoff times.

FAQ

Does this include weekends?

No. Weekends are skipped because they are not business days.

Does this include U.S. federal holidays?

No. Observed U.S. federal holidays are treated as non-business days.

Will this match my payroll provider exactly?

Not always. Providers may have cutoff times and bank settlement rules that affect timing.

What if the estimated date lands on a holiday?

The calculator moves the date forward to the next available business day.

Is this a guarantee of when employees get paid?

No. This is an estimate for planning. Always confirm with your payroll provider.
