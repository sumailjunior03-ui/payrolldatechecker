What counts as a business day?
A business day is Monday–Friday excluding observed U.S. federal holidays. If a fixed-date holiday lands on a weekend, the observed day is used (Fri/Mon).

When This Calculator Is Useful

Use this calculator when you need to plan payroll submission or payment timing around business days. It is helpful for HR teams, payroll administrators, and business owners who need to confirm submit-by dates to ensure employees are paid on a specific target date, or to predict when funds will arrive given a known submit date and provider lead-time.

What This Calculator Does

This calculator takes a target date and a lead-time in business days, then counts backward or forward across U.S. business days to return the corresponding submit date or pay date. It accounts for weekends and observed U.S. federal holidays. You can switch between two modes: finding the submit date from a known pay date, or finding the pay date from a known submit date.

Calculation Methodology

Business days are defined as Monday through Friday, excluding observed U.S. federal holidays. When a fixed-date holiday falls on a Saturday, the preceding Friday is the observed holiday. When it falls on a Sunday, the following Monday is the observed holiday. The calculator steps through calendar days one at a time, skipping non-business days, until the specified number of business-day lead-time intervals have been traversed.
